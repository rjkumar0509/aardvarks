Welcome to the Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Club

About the Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Club

Founded in 1973, the Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Football Club has been a pillar of New Mexico's rugby community for over five decades. Known for our tenacity on the field and our camaraderie off it, we’ve helped grow the sport of rugby union throughout the Southwest United States.

Our Mission

Our mission is simple but powerful: to promote the sport of rugby while fostering teamwork, discipline, and respect both on and off the pitch. We welcome athletes of all backgrounds and skill levels who want to challenge themselves and contribute to something bigger.

Our History

Since our founding in 1973, the Aardvarks have built a long-standing tradition of excellence and community involvement. From grassroots rugby outreach programs to hosting regional tournaments, the Aardvarks are deeply invested in rugby development in New Mexico.

Club Achievements

  • Multiple regional rugby championships
  • Consistent playoff appearances in Southwest rugby leagues
  • Strong alumni support network across the U.S.
  • Host and competitor in the annual High Desert Rugby Tournament

As one of the longest-running men’s rugby clubs in New Mexico, we take pride in our rich history, competitive edge, and community-driven spirit. Whether you're a seasoned player or a new fan, there's a place for you with the Albuquerque Aardvarks RFC.

SafeSport Commitment

Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Club is fully committed to providing a safe, positive, and respectful environment for all participants. We proudly follow and enforce the policies and procedures outlined by USA Rugby's SafeSport Program.

This includes mandatory training, reporting protocols, and strict prevention measures to protect players, coaches, officials, and supporters from abuse, misconduct, or harassment.

To learn more or report a concern, please visit the USA Rugby SafeSport page.

USA Rugby Player Registration (CIPP) – Mandatory

If you want to play with the Albuquerque Aardvarks, you must be fully registered with USA Rugby through the Club and Individual Participation Program (CIPP). No CIPP = No training, no matches, no exceptions.

How to Create an Account with USA Rugby

  1. Go to usa.rugby/membership.
  2. Click on “Sign Up” and follow the instructions to create your Rugby Xplorer account.
  3. Use a valid email and strong password you’ll remember for future logins.
  4. Create AccountVideo Tutorial.

How to Register as a Player with the Albuquerque Aardvark Rugby Club for a Season

  1. Log into Rugby Xplorer.
  2. Search for Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Club when prompted to select a club.
  3. Select your role: Player, and choose the appropriate division or age group.
  4. Complete the registration and payment process.
  5. Register Player for whole seasonVideo Tutorial.

Reminder: This registration covers you for the full season and is required before participating in any team activity.

How to Register/CIPP with USA Rugby for Weekly Tournaments/Games

If you're only joining for specific games or tournaments, you still need a valid CIPP registration. USA Rugby does not allow any exceptions.

  1. Follow the full registration process listed above.
  2. Even short-term participation requires full compliance with USA Rugby's membership rules.
  3. Register Player for a week game Video Tutorial.

No partial registration. No guest play. Full CIPP or you're not on the field.

How to Transfer CIPP from Another Club to Albuquerque Aardvarks Rugby Club

  1. Log in to your Rugby Xplorer account and try to register with the new club.
  2. The system will block the request and prompt you to submit a transfer request. This will send a notification to your home club and local union, informing each of your intent to transfer.
  3. If you have no outstanding financial or disciplinary issues, the home club and local union will approve the transfer.
  4. Once approved (or denied) for the transfer, you will receive an email notification confirming the transfer has been approved(or denied).
  5. If approved by both the home club and local union, you must go back to your account and continue to register to the new club. If changing Geogrpahic Unions, you may have to pay their membership fee.
  6. At this point you will appear on the Squad Member list of your new club

If you need help, reach out to the club at aardvarksrugby@gmail.com. But don’t expect to play if you’re not registered.
